The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom

Game Description

The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom is a 2023 Action Adventure game by Nintendo. You play as Link, as he fights to prevent Ganondorf from destroying Hyrule.

The game is played from a Third-person perspective. It features a large, open world for the player to explore. One interesting feature is the non-linear progression. This means you can choose which order you can complete the story in, even rushing straight to the final boss! Though it may not be advised...

Quick Tips

  • Use your paraglider to quickly traverse the world, and to escape from dangerous situations.
  • Experiment with different weapon combinations to find what works best for you. Some weapons have special abilities that can be useful in certain situations.
  • Don't be afraid to explore! The world of Hyrule is full of secrets and hidden treasures waiting to be discovered.

Strategy Guide

Whilst Tears of the Kingdom has quite a strong opening tutorial, there are still some things the game doesnt tell you. Here are some tips and tricks, and maybe even some features you may have missed!

Finding caves

If you're out exploring, and you encounter a Blupee, there is a high chance that there is a cave nearby! When you approach a blupee, it will dart off towards the closest cave entrance. Make sure to chase them!

If you look at the map, an icon will appear on each explored cave, which means you won't waste time searching the same cave twice

Using Bubbul Gems

Bubbul gems can be accquired from the Bubbulfrogs that can be found lurking on cave walls. Defeating a bubbulfrog will drop a Bubbul Gem. Defeating the frog will also mark the cave on the map.

These gems can be traded for powerful gear and unique masks, such as the Bokoblin mask, Moblin mask or the Lynel mask. Trading 46 gems will get you the Mystic armour set. Finding all 147 Bubbul gems will get you the Kotlin's fabric modification for your paraglider.

Before you can trade these gems, you need to find Kotlin. He can be found, usually at night, near areas such as the Elden Mountain Skyview Tower, or the Snowfield Stable. He is marked by a purple balloon.

Weaponising Ascend

Ascend is an ability that allows Link to rise up through ceilings, hillsides and even certain enemies. Whilst this is primarily a traversal ability, a very powerful one at that, it has some surprising uses during combat too!

When fighting a Stone Talus, try running beneath it and using ascend. You'll phase through it and be able to access the ore deposit weakpoint.

The same technique can be used when fighting a Flux Construct. If you find yourself underneath a Flux Construct, use Ascend to phase through it and strike its weakpoint.
