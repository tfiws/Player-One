Battlefield 6

Game Description

Battlefield 6 is a 2025 military first-person shooter developed by Battlefield Studios, and published by EA.

Quick Tips

Here are some quick tips to help you stay on track with Battlefield 6:

  • Always keep an eye on your minimap to stay aware of enemy positions.
  • Use cover effectively to minimize damage from enemy fire.
  • Coordinate with your team to execute successful strategies.

Strategy Guide

Battlefield 6 has many ways to play. But, to truly master the game, you need to understand the ins and outs of each class, not only so you can play them effectively, but counter them too.

Assault

The Assault class focuses on charging the enemy with Adrenaline, pushing the frontline forward with deploy beacons and capturing objectives with 2x capture speed. The class is proficient with assualt rifles, giving them a faster shoulder speed. A strong rifle to use would be the Kord 6P67, suitable for both long and medium range encounters, with high stability and solid range.

Most players overlook the Assault Ladder gadget. At first, it may seem like a boring tool when compared to a grenade launcher, but when utilized correctly, it can be incredibly effective. You can use one of most players' greatest weakness against them; nobody looks up!

Engineer

As an engineer, you need to focus on providing support to friendly vehicles by using your repair tool. Many players focus on destroying enemy armour, and whilst this is a key aspect of the class, it is just as important to keep your vehicles healthy and fully stocked.

Engineers have access to powerful rocket launchers, that can be used to destroy enemy vehicles. However, many players get tunnel vison trying to destroy a single vehicle. Even putting a few rounds into an enemy tank will hinder its movement speed dramatically, forcing it to retreat and repair, buying your team valuable time.

As engineers are aimed towards vehicle support, they are proficient in SMG use. A strong SMG to use is the MP5. This weapon's low recoil means it excels at close to medium range engagements.

Support

Support is a difficult class to master. Effective support requires good awareness of the battlefield. Before going for revives, make sure to survey the area, ensuring that your revived teammate will be safe. A good method is to use Smoke Grenades to provide cover while reviving teammates, blocking enemy sightlines. Fully charging your Defibrilators will give your teammate full health on revive.

Support players have access to LMGs, which are powerful weapons that can suppress enemies and control areas. A strong LMG to use is the DRS-IAR, which has a high rate of fire and good accuracy, making it effective for suppressing enemies and providing cover fire.

Recon

Recon is a confusing case. The most popular approach is to find a high vantage point, spot enemies and maybe snipe one or two. However, this is not massivley beneficial to your team. Not only are you not providing valuable intel, you are also not playing the objective.

To be an effective recon, don't just focus on sniping. Try to gather intelligence about enemy positions and movements. This can be done using your Drone. This is a powerful gadge that allows you remotely spot enemy infantry, gadgets and vehicles, without putting yourself in danger.

Recons are proficient with Sniper Rifles. They have the unique ability to deny an enemy revive on a headshot. Use this ability to target supports, preventing them from reviving more enemies.

Map Guide

Manhattan Bridge

Popular sniper spots:

  • On the bridge, overlooking the central area
  • On the rooftops of the buildings on the right side of the map
  • On the rooftops of the buildings on the left side of the map

Unexpected flank routes:

  • From the left side to the right side, using the bridge as a pathway
  • From the right side to the left side, utilizing the rooftops for cover
